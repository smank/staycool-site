Two instruments in one plugin

Classic — the hardware

A faithful 2A03: two pulse channels, triangle, noise, and DPCM sample playback, plus the optional VRC6 expansion for two more pulses and a sawtooth. Bandlimited so it stays clean at any pitch, with the nonlinear DAC curve that gives the chip its bite. This is the real 8-bit voice — not a sample of it.

Modern — the synth

When you want to step outside the hardware, flip to a full polyphonic synth built from the same waveforms: dual oscillators, ADSR, unison with detune, portamento, and per-voice MPE pitch bend. Chiptune timbre, modern playability — pads, leads and stacks the original chip could never hold.

What you get to do with it

▦

Program lines that play themselves

A tracker-style step sequencer on every channel — 16 steps across volume, pitch and duty lanes. Hold one note and Cartridge plays the riff: basslines, arps, wobbles, the works. Draw straight into the grid, sync it to your host, and it's captured in your project.

Steal from the classics

Drop in a .vgm or .vgz game rip and Cartridge pulls each instrument out as a preset — volume, pitch and duty envelopes intact. The exact voices from decades of chiptune, ready to play from your keyboard.

Questions

What do I need to run it?

macOS 11 or later, Apple Silicon or Intel (it's a universal binary). Load it as VST3, AU, or CLAP in any modern DAW, or run the standalone app on its own.

Which DAWs does it work in?

Anything that hosts VST3, AU, or CLAP — Logic, Ableton Live, Reaper, Bitwig, FL Studio, Cubase, and the rest. AU is included specifically for Logic and GarageBand.

Do I need to know chiptune to use it?

No. Start from a preset, play your keyboard, and you're making 8-bit music. Hover any control for a plain-English description, and the built-in Help screen explains the chip, the jargon, and the shortcuts. Go as deep as you want — or don't.

Is it really the 8-bit chip, or samples?

The real thing. Cartridge synthesises the 2A03's voices in real time — bandlimited so they stay clean at any pitch — including the nonlinear DAC curve that gives the chip its character. Nothing is sampled from a console.

What's the licence?

The binary you buy comes with a clean commercial licence — use it on your records with no strings. The source is also available under AGPLv3 to commercial-licence holders on request. Buy once, own it; no subscription.

Windows or Linux?

The macOS build is what's signed, notarised and sold today. Windows and Linux builds exist and are on the roadmap — email me if that's you and I'll keep you posted.
